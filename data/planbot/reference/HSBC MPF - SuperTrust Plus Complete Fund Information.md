Important Fee Notes
Management fees include fees paid to the Trustee, Custodian, Platform Company, Investment Manager, Investment Agent, and MPF Scheme Provider.
For Core Accumulation Fund and Age 65 Plus Fund, management fees are subject to a statutory daily limit of 0.75% p.a.
"Other expenses" (audit, professional fees, etc.) are deducted separately. For DIS funds, recurrent out-of-pocket expenses are capped at 0.10% p.a. from 1 July 2026.
Preferential management fee rates may be offered to certain members via "special bonus units."
Any changes to waived fees require at least 12 months' prior notice.

4. Key Risks
General Investment Risk
The value of investments can go down as well as up.
Past performance is not indicative of future results.
Original investment amount may not be recouped.
Guaranteed Fund Risk
Guarantee applies only upon specific conditions (retirement, death, total incapacity, etc.).
Subject to credit risk of HSBC Life (International) Limited.
Members who withdraw before meeting guarantee conditions are subject to market fluctuations.
Default Investment Strategy (DIS) Risks
Asset allocation is determined solely by age, not personal circumstances or market conditions.
De-risking occurs automatically regardless of market performance.
The strategy may not be suitable for all members.
Emerging Markets Risk
Funds investing in Asia Pacific, China, and other emerging markets face higher political, economic, and liquidity risks.
May be subject to currency controls, less stringent accounting standards, and settlement delays.
Currency Risk
Fluctuations in exchange rates can affect returns.
Each fund maintains a minimum 30% HKD exposure.
Interest Rate and Credit Risk
Bond funds are susceptible to interest rate changes.
Issuer defaults or credit rating downgrades may affect bond values.
Financial Derivatives Risk
Some underlying funds use derivatives for hedging or efficient portfolio management.
Derivatives can be volatile and involve leverage.
