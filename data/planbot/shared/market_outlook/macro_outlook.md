## 1. Interest Rate & Fixed Income Outlook (The Primary Focus)

The dominant narrative in fixed income over the last two weeks is the **aggressive unwinding of easing expectations**, particularly in the United States.

```
[Geopolitical Bottlenecks (Hormuz)] ➔ [Energy Price Spikes (Oil $90–$120)] ➔ [Sticky April/May Inflation] ➔ [Central Banks Shift to "Simultaneous Hold"]

```

### United States (Federal Reserve)

* **The Pivot Away from Cuts:** Recent Federal Reserve commentary has turned starkly hawkish. In late May, Governor Christopher Waller noted that with core PCE inflation tracking around 3.3% year-over-year and headline PCE near 3.8% (boosted by energy spikes), the Fed may need to remove its "easing bias" entirely.
* **Rate Path:** Wall Street is rapidly pricing out rate cuts for the remainder of 2026. Major institutions (such as J.P. Morgan and ABN Amro) now anticipate the Fed will maintain a **"simultaneous hold"** at current levels. With Kevin Warsh stepping into focus as a major leadership influence, the policy tilt emphasizes keeping rates restrictive until employment fragilities or substantial disinflation force their hand.

### Europe & the UK (ECB & BoE)

* **Policy Divergence:** Unlike the US, the Eurozone is experiencing a sharper growth slowdown (Q1 2026 GDP projections down to 0.8% for the Euro Area). While the European Central Bank (ECB) had previously cut rates eight times, the sudden Middle East energy crisis is limiting further easing. The ECB is expected to pause or severely taper cuts to avoid importing energy-driven inflation, though European government bonds continue to technically outperform US Treasuries due to weaker domestic growth demand.

### Japan (BOJ)

* **The Outlier:** While the West enters a prolonged hold, Japan remains on a tightening trajectory, looking to normalize its yield curve as domestic structural adjustments take hold.
