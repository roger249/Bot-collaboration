# Needs

The file provides some samples of the needs of an investor that will drive the investor to buy products.  The needs can be divided into two categories.

1. Quality of the current investment of the portfolio is deteriorating.  Bond being downgraded.  Growth momentum of the equity sector or geographic location subsides.  

2. Demographic of the client such as retirement or children education.

Those need could be estimated from client profile. For example, a client of age 55 will have retirement needs, or a client having two kids of age 10-15 will need university funding.

These needs are further qualified in the following dimension for product matching.
- Time horizon.  How long we need the fund.
- Return.  What's the target return.  Score 1-5.  The higher the return required, the larger the score is.

Below table shows the needs sample.

| Investment Objective | Typical Horizon (y) | Return (1-5) | Volatility (1-5) | The Strategic Logic |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| University Fund / Mortgage down payment | 10–15 | 3 | 2 | Horizon-Locked: High certainty is achievable through balanced funds over 10 years. |
| Business Owner | 1–2 | 1 | 1 | Operating Buffer: Certainty must be near 100% (Contractual) due to the short horizon. |
| Retirement (Accumulation) | \>7 | 5 | 5 | Growth Dominance: Can trade liquidity for returns; time creates the certainty. |
| Retirement (Distribution) | Ongoing | 3 | 2 | Cash Flow: Needs a high certainty of yield/coupons to fund monthly life. |
| Estate/Legacy | 30+ | 5 | 5 | Multi-Gen: Max volatility is ignored to harvest the highest long-term returns. |
| Emergency Fund | 0.5-1 | 1 | 1 | Immediate Need: No time for mean reversion; requires absolute price stability. |
| Tax Liability Reserve/Loan | <1 | 1 | 1 | Fixed-date obligation; matching duration is critical, not return. |



"Bridge to social security" is ignored for the moment as we need to account for the difference of each country.
