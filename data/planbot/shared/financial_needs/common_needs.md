# Goal-Based Investment Framework

This framework applies a **goal-based investing** approach: each client objective is treated as a distinct investment goal with its own time horizon, return target, and certainty requirement. Rather than optimizing a single aggregated portfolio, goal-based investing allocates assets to satisfy each goal independently, producing more precise product-to-goal alignment.

Goals are identified through two primary triggers:

1.  **Portfolio Hygiene:** Addressing deteriorating asset quality (e.g., credit downgrades, slowing sector momentum, or unfavorable geographic shifts).
2.  **Life-Stage Milestones:** Demographic-driven requirements such as retirement planning or educational funding, often inferred from the client's age and family structure.

### Goal Dimensions
Every investment goal is characterized by three dimensions that together define the appropriate risk-return profile and product fit:

| Dimension         | Scale | Description                                                                                                                                                                    |
| :---------------- | :---- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Time Horizon**  | Years | The duration until the capital is needed. Shorter horizons demand higher certainty; longer horizons can absorb interim volatility.                                             |
| **Target Return** | 1–5   | Required growth intensity. 1 = capital preservation; 5 = aggressive compounding.                                                                                               |
| **Certainty**     | 1–5   | Required probability of meeting the goal at maturity. 1 = speculative (long-horizon goals can accept uncertainty); 5 = guaranteed (near-term obligations must not fall short). |

### Common Investment Goals
The following table maps typical client goals to their dimensions and the strategic logic driving product selection:

| Investment Goal               | Time Horizon (y) | Target Return (1–5) | Certainty (1–5) | Strategic Logic                                                                                                                 |
| :---------------------------- | :--------------- | :------------------ | :-------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **University Fund**           | 10–15            | 3                   | 4               | **Horizon-Locked:** A decade-plus horizon allows balanced assets to smooth volatility while delivering high terminal certainty. |
| **Mortgage Down Payment**     | 3–5              | 3                   | 4               | **Certainty-Tilt:** The defined target and high consequence of shortfall prioritize balanced growth with greater downside protection. |
| **Business Operating Buffer** | 1–2              | 1                   | 5               | **Capital Integrity:** Near-100% certainty required; the short horizon leaves no room for market recovery.                      |
| **Retirement (Accumulation)** | 7+               | 5                   | 2               | **Growth Dominance:** Long-term compounding justifies high volatility and lower short-term certainty.                           |
| **Retirement (Distribution)** | Ongoing          | 3                   | 4               | **Liability Matching:** Steady yield and capital preservation to sustain regular cash outflows throughout decumulation.         |
| **Estate / Legacy**           | 25+              | 5                   | 1               | **Multi-Generational:** Extreme horizon renders short-term volatility irrelevant; prioritizes maximum long-run appreciation.    |
| **Emergency Fund**            | <1               | 1                   | 5               | **Instant Liquidity:** Absolute stability required; serves as a safety net for the client's standard of living.                 |
| **Tax / Loan Reserve**        | <1               | 1                   | 5               | **Fixed Obligation:** Funds must be fully intact by the payment date; return is secondary to total certainty.                   |
